WHAT IS THIS OIL?
Silicone diffusion pump oil is a special high-purity silicone fluid that sits inside the diffusion pump of your metalliser. When heated, it creates a high-speed vapour jet that physically sweeps air and gas molecules out of the vacuum chamber — creating the deep vacuum needed to deposit aluminium on film.

🏭 First — Why Does a Metalliser Need Such Deep Vacuum?

Think about what a vacuum metalliser is doing. It is taking a solid aluminium wire, melting it at about 1400°C in small ceramic boats, and evaporating it — turning solid metal into a cloud of aluminium vapour. That vapour then travels across the chamber and lands on the BOPET or BOPP film passing over it, forming a mirror-like coating that is only a few hundred angstroms thick (about 1000 times thinner than a human hair).

For this to work, the aluminium vapour must travel in a straight line from the boat to the film without hitting any air molecules on the way. At normal atmospheric pressure, an aluminium atom would collide with an air molecule within a fraction of a millimetre — the coating would be useless. To get the aluminium vapour to travel the full distance cleanly, the pressure inside the chamber must be reduced to roughly 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ Torr — about one ten-millionth of normal atmospheric pressure. Creating and holding that vacuum is the only job of the pump system. And the silicone diffusion pump oil is the critical fluid that makes it possible.

⚙️ The Three-Pump System — A Relay Race

No single pump can take a chamber from atmospheric pressure to 10⁻⁵ Torr in one step. It takes three pumps working together in sequence — like three runners in a relay race, each handing off to the next.

The three-pump relay: Rotary pump brings pressure down to ~1 Torr. Roots booster takes it to ~10⁻² Torr. Diffusion pump (running on silicone oil) takes it the rest of the way to 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ Torr — process vacuum.

Pump 1 — Rotary Vane Pump (the roughing pump). This is the first runner. It is a mechanical pump, similar in concept to a car engine but designed to pump air instead of fuel. It uses a different oil — a rotary pump mineral oil (not the silicone oil we are discussing). Its job is to bring the chamber pressure down from atmospheric (~760 Torr) to a rough vacuum of about 1 Torr. At this point it hands off to the next pump.

Pump 2 — Roots Booster Pump (the blower). The second runner. A Roots blower uses two figure-8 shaped rotors spinning at high speed to push large volumes of gas out quickly. It takes the pressure from ~1 Torr down to around 10⁻² Torr. It uses yet another oil — a booster pump oil. Different from both the rotary pump oil and the diffusion pump oil. Now the third pump takes over.

Pump 3 — Diffusion Pump (the finisher). The last and most important runner for deep vacuum. This pump has no moving parts at all — no rotors, no vanes, no pistons. It works entirely using the silicone oil (CVC-4 / SV-Sigma). The oil is heated at the bottom of the pump, vaporises, shoots upward through a series of jet nozzles at very high speed, collides with the remaining gas molecules in the chamber, drags them downward to the backing pump, and then condenses back on the water-cooled walls of the pump to be recycled and heated again. This continuous cycle of vaporise → jet → condense → recycle is what takes the pressure all the way down to 10⁻⁴ Torr and holds it there during the entire metallising run.

🛢️ What Exactly is Silicone Diffusion Pump Oil — And Why Silicone?

The silicone oil used in diffusion pumps is a highly purified silicone compound called tetramethyltetraphenyl trisiloxane. That is the technical name — but what it means in practice is a very clean, very stable liquid that has properties no other oil can match for this application.

Inside the diffusion pump: silicone oil is heated (bottom), vaporises up through the jet nozzles, sweeps gas molecules down to the backing pump, and condenses back on the cooled walls. No moving parts. Just oil and physics.

Why must it be silicone — and not a regular mineral oil or engine oil?

1. Extremely low vapour pressure. At room temperature, silicone diffusion pump oil barely evaporates at all. This is critical because the oil is inside the vacuum chamber's pumping system. If the oil had a high vapour pressure, its molecules would escape into the vacuum chamber and contaminate the film being coated. Mineral oils have vapour pressures that are far too high for this application. Silicone oil stays where it is supposed to stay.

2. High thermal stability. The oil is heated to around 200–220°C inside the pump boiler — repeatedly, every single production run. A mineral oil at this temperature would crack, turn black, and decompose within weeks. Silicone diffusion pump oil handles this temperature cycle for 1.5 to 2 years without significant degradation under normal operating conditions.

3. Chemical inertness. The oil does not react with the metal surfaces of the pump (steel, chromium, aluminium), with the gases being pumped out (nitrogen, argon, water vapour from the film), or with the elastomer seals in the pump. It is chemically unreactive — it just does its job and nothing else.

4. Very low backstreaming. Backstreaming is when oil vapour migrates in the wrong direction — from the diffusion pump into the vacuum chamber — and deposits on the film being coated or on the chamber walls. Even tiny amounts of backstreamed oil will appear as adhesion failures, poor ink receptivity, or haze on metallised film. Silicone diffusion pump oil — especially the high-grade variants like CVC-4 / SV-Sigma — has an extremely low backstreaming rate. This keeps the vacuum chamber clean and the film quality consistent.

🔍 Three Types of Oil in Your Metalliser — Don't Mix Them Up

This is the mistake that happens more than anything else. A metalliser uses three different oils in three different pumps — and all three are completely different products. Using the wrong oil in the wrong pump damages the equipment.

1️⃣
Rotary Pump Oil (Roughing Pump)
Mineral-based or synthetic hydrocarbon oil. Examples: Supervac SV-77, Edwards Ultragrade 19, Inland 19. Purpose: lubrication + sealing in the mechanical vane pump. Do NOT put diffusion pump silicone oil here.
2️⃣
Booster / Roots Pump Oil
Special booster pump oil — viscosity matched to the Roots blower. Examples: Supervac SV-Booster, Leybold LVO 100 series. Purpose: lubrication of the blower gearbox. Separate reservoir from rotary pump.
⭐ THIS IS CVC-4
3️⃣
Diffusion Pump Oil (Silicone Oil)
High-purity silicone fluid — CVC-4 / DC-704 / SV-Sigma grade. No lubrication role — it is the pumping mechanism. Purpose: create and maintain the deep vacuum (10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ Torr) needed for aluminium deposition. This is what this blog is about.

🩺 How to Know When Your Diffusion Pump Oil Needs Changing

There is no single fixed interval for changing diffusion pump oil — it depends on how many hours the machine runs, what film is being processed, how clean the chamber is maintained, and whether the oil has ever been accidentally exposed to air while hot. What you can rely on is a set of signs that tell you the oil is degrading and needs attention.

Oil colour is your first indicator. Fresh silicone oil is clear and water-like. Pale yellow = still OK. Light brown = watch closely. Dark brown or black = change now before pump damage occurs.

Sign 1 — Vacuum takes longer to reach process level. If your pump used to reach 10⁻⁴ Torr in a certain time and now takes noticeably longer, the oil's pumping efficiency has dropped. This is usually the first sign of oil degradation.

Sign 2 — Ultimate vacuum is worse than before. If your vacuum gauge is showing a higher number than usual (less vacuum) during a run — even with the same chamber load and film — the oil is losing its ability to maintain deep vacuum.

Sign 3 — Oil colour has changed. Fresh silicone diffusion pump oil is clear, transparent, and odourless — like water but slightly more viscous. As it degrades it turns pale yellow, then light brown, then dark brown or black. Dark brown or black oil must be changed immediately. Do not wait.

Sign 4 — Film quality issues that cannot be explained otherwise. If metallised film is showing patchy adhesion, low optical density in spots, or haze that was not there before — and you have already ruled out aluminium wire quality, boat condition, and film pre-treatment — backstreamed degraded oil contaminating the chamber is a serious suspect.

Sign 5 — The pump has been air-vented while hot. If a power cut, emergency stop, or operator error caused the diffusion pump to be exposed to atmospheric air while still at operating temperature, the oil is likely oxidised and degraded. Change it regardless of how old it is.

Under normal operating conditions with a well-maintained machine, most plants in India change diffusion pump oil every 18 to 24 months. But signs 1 to 5 above override any calendar schedule.

⚠️ Three Mistakes That Damage Your Diffusion Pump

1. Using rotary pump oil or booster oil in the diffusion pump. It sounds obvious, but it happens. A new operator, an unlabelled drum, an urgent maintenance situation — and the wrong oil goes into the diffusion pump. Rotary pump mineral oil has a much higher vapour pressure than silicone oil. Inside the diffusion pump, it will vaporise excessively, contaminate the vacuum chamber, deposit on the film, and can thermally decompose into a black tar that clogs the jet nozzles. Recovery requires complete pump disassembly and cleaning — days of downtime.

2. Mixing old oil with new oil during a top-up. Diffusion pump oil level drops slightly over time due to minor losses. The instinct is to top up with fresh oil rather than do a full change. This is acceptable if the existing oil is clean and in good condition. But if the existing oil is already degraded (yellow-brown), adding fresh oil on top does not restore it — the degraded oil contaminates the new oil and you end up with a full chamber of mixed, compromised fluid. If the oil needs attention, drain completely first.

3. Starting the diffusion pump heater before the backing pump has achieved rough vacuum. The diffusion pump must only be heated and run once the rotary pump has already achieved a rough vacuum below ~0.5 Torr. If the diffusion pump is started at atmospheric or near-atmospheric pressure, the hot silicone oil is exposed to oxygen and will oxidise rapidly — turning dark brown or black within one run. This is the fastest way to destroy a charge of diffusion pump oil and is purely a procedural error.

Share this with your maintenance team. These three mistakes cost plants thousands in pump repairs and days of downtime — all preventable.

🏭 पहले — Metalliser को इतना Deep Vacuum क्यों चाहिए?

सोचिए metalliser actually क्या कर रहा है। वो solid aluminium wire को ~1400°C पर छोटे ceramic boats में melt कर रहा है और evaporate कर रहा है — solid metal को aluminium vapour के cloud में बदल रहा है। वो vapour chamber के across travel करता है और उस BOPET या BOPP film पर land करता है जो उसके ऊपर से pass हो रही है — एक mirror-like coating बनाता है जो सिर्फ़ कुछ सौ angstroms thick होती है (human hair से लगभग 1000 गुना पतली)।

इसके लिए ज़रूरी है कि aluminium vapour boat से film तक straight line में travel करे — रास्ते में कोई air molecule न मिले। Normal atmospheric pressure पर एक aluminium atom एक fraction of a millimetre में ही air molecule से collide हो जाता — coating बेकार होती। Aluminium vapour को cleanly पूरी distance travel करवाने के लिए chamber का pressure लगभग 10⁻⁴ से 10⁻⁵ Torr तक reduce करना होता है — normal atmospheric pressure का लगभग एक करोड़वाँ हिस्सा। इस vacuum को create और hold करना ही pump system का काम है। और silicone diffusion pump oil वो critical fluid है जो ये possible बनाती है।

⚙️ तीन-Pump System — एक Relay Race

कोई एक pump अकेले chamber को atmospheric pressure से 10⁻⁵ Torr तक एक step में नहीं ले जा सकता। तीन pumps को sequence में मिलकर काम करना पड़ता है — जैसे relay race में तीन runners, एक-दूसरे को handoff करते हुए।

तीन-pump relay: Rotary pump pressure ~1 Torr तक लाता है। Roots booster ~10⁻² Torr तक। Diffusion pump (silicone oil पर running) 10⁻⁴ से 10⁻⁵ Torr तक — process vacuum।

Pump 1 — Rotary Vane Pump (roughing pump)। ये पहला runner है। Mechanical pump है — जो air pump करने के लिए design किया गया है। ये एक अलग oil use करता है — rotary pump mineral oil (जो silicone oil नहीं है)। इसका काम atmospheric pressure (~760 Torr) से ~1 Torr के rough vacuum तक लाना है।

Pump 2 — Roots Booster Pump। दूसरा runner। Figure-8 shaped rotors high speed पर spin करके large volumes of gas quickly बाहर push करते हैं। Pressure ~1 Torr से ~10⁻² Torr तक जाता है। ये भी अलग oil use करता है — booster pump oil। Rotary pump oil और diffusion pump oil दोनों से अलग।

Pump 3 — Diffusion Pump (finisher)। Deep vacuum के लिए सबसे important runner। इस pump में कोई moving parts नहीं — कोई rotors, vanes, pistons नहीं। ये पूरी तरह silicone oil (CVC-4 / SV-Sigma) से काम करता है। Oil pump के bottom पर heat होती है, vaporise होती है, jet nozzles से ऊपर shoot करती है, chamber के बचे gas molecules को collide करके नीचे backing pump की तरफ drag करती है, और pump की water-cooled walls पर condense होकर वापस recycle होती है। ये continuous cycle — vaporise → jet → condense → recycle — pressure को 10⁻⁴ Torr तक ले जाता है और पूरे metallising run में hold करता है।

🛢️ Silicone Diffusion Pump Oil Actually क्या है — और Silicone ही क्यों?

Diffusion pump में use होने वाला silicone oil एक highly purified compound है जिसे tetramethyltetraphenyl trisiloxane कहते हैं। Technical नाम से ज़्यादा ज़रूरी है ये जानना कि इसमें ऐसी properties हैं जो इस application के लिए कोई दूसरा oil match नहीं कर सकता।

Diffusion pump के अंदर: silicone oil heat होती है (नीचे), jet nozzles से vaporise होकर ऊपर जाती है, gas molecules को नीचे sweep करती है, cooled walls पर condense होती है। कोई moving parts नहीं। सिर्फ़ oil और physics।

Regular mineral oil या engine oil क्यों नहीं?

1. Extremely low vapour pressure। Room temperature पर silicone diffusion pump oil practically evaporate ही नहीं होती। ये critical है क्योंकि oil vacuum chamber के pumping system के अंदर है। अगर oil की high vapour pressure होती, उसके molecules vacuum chamber में escape होकर film को contaminate करते। Mineral oils की vapour pressure इसके लिए बहुत high है।

2. High thermal stability। Oil pump boiler में ~200–220°C तक heat होती है — बार-बार, हर production run में। इस temperature पर mineral oil weeks में crack होकर black हो जाता। Silicone diffusion pump oil normal operating conditions में 1.5 से 2 साल तक बिना significant degradation के ये temperature cycle handle करती है।

3. Chemical inertness। Oil pump के metal surfaces (steel, chromium, aluminium), pumped gases (nitrogen, argon, film से water vapour), या pump के elastomer seals के साथ react नहीं करती। Chemically unreactive है — बस अपना काम करती है।

4. Very low backstreaming। Backstreaming तब होती है जब oil vapour wrong direction में migrate करती है — diffusion pump से vacuum chamber में — और metallised film या chamber walls पर deposit हो जाती है। थोड़ी सी भी backstreamed oil adhesion failures, poor ink receptivity, या film पर haze दिखाएगी। Silicone diffusion pump oil (खासकर CVC-4 / SV-Sigma grade) में extremely low backstreaming rate है।

सभी नाम mapped — कौन बनाता है, क्या replace करता है, India में क्या order करें। Purchase team के साथ share करें।

🔍 Metalliser में तीन अलग-अलग Oils — इन्हें Mix मत करना

ये सबसे ज़्यादा होने वाली mistake है। Metalliser में तीन अलग pumps में तीन completely अलग oils होती हैं — और तीनों entirely different products हैं।

1️⃣
Rotary Pump Oil (Roughing Pump)
Mineral-based या synthetic hydrocarbon oil। Examples: Supervac SV-77, Edwards Ultragrade 19। Purpose: mechanical vane pump में lubrication + sealing। यहाँ diffusion pump silicone oil मत डालें।
2️⃣
Booster / Roots Pump Oil
Special booster pump oil। Examples: Supervac SV-Booster। Purpose: Roots blower gearbox lubrication। Rotary pump से separate reservoir होता है।
⭐ यही है CVC-4
3️⃣
Diffusion Pump Oil (Silicone Oil)
High-purity silicone fluid — CVC-4 / DC-704 / SV-Sigma grade। कोई lubrication role नहीं — ये खुद ही pumping mechanism है। Purpose: aluminium deposition के लिए deep vacuum (10⁻⁴ से 10⁻⁵ Torr) create और maintain करना। यही blog इसी के बारे में है।

🩺 Diffusion Pump Oil कब बदलनी है — 5 Signs

Oil change का कोई fixed interval नहीं होता — machine कितने hours run करती है, कौन सी film process होती है, chamber कितना clean रहता है, और क्या oil कभी hot रहते air से expose हुई है — सब depend करता है। लेकिन ये 5 signs clearly बताते हैं कि oil degrade हो रही है।

Oil का colour पहला indicator है। Fresh silicone oil clear और water जैसी होती है। Pale yellow = अभी OK। Light brown = closely watch करें। Dark brown या black = अभी बदलें।

Sign 1 — Vacuum process level तक पहुँचने में ज़्यादा time लग रहा है। पहले जितने time में 10⁻⁴ Torr आता था, अब noticeably ज़्यादा लग रहा है — oil की pumping efficiency drop हो गई है।

Sign 2 — Ultimate vacuum पहले से worse है। Same chamber load और film के साथ भी vacuum gauge usual से higher number (less vacuum) दिखा रहा है — oil deep vacuum maintain करने की ability खो रही है।

Sign 3 — Oil का colour बदल गया है। Fresh silicone diffusion pump oil clear, transparent, और odourless होती है — पानी जैसी लेकिन थोड़ी ज़्यादा viscous। Degrade होने पर pale yellow, फिर light brown, फिर dark brown या black होती है। Dark brown या black oil को तुरंत बदलें।

Sign 4 — Film quality issues जो otherwise explain न हों। Metallised film पर patchy adhesion, spots में low optical density, या unexplained haze — और aluminium wire, boat condition, film pre-treatment सब check हो चुके हों — तो backstreamed degraded oil serious suspect है।

Sign 5 — Pump hot रहते air से expose हुई हो। Power cut, emergency stop, या operator error से diffusion pump गर्म रहते atmospheric air से expose हुई हो तो oil oxidised और degraded है। उम्र चाहे कोई भी हो, बदल दें।

Normal operating conditions में well-maintained machine पर India के ज़्यादातर plants diffusion pump oil हर 18 से 24 महीने में बदलते हैं। लेकिन ऊपर के 5 signs किसी भी calendar schedule को override करते हैं।

⚠️ तीन Mistakes जो Diffusion Pump Damage करती हैं

1. Diffusion pump में rotary pump oil या booster oil डालना। होता है — नया operator, unlabelled drum, urgent maintenance। Rotary pump mineral oil की vapour pressure silicone oil से बहुत high है। Diffusion pump में ये excessive vaporise होगी, vacuum chamber contaminate करेगी, film पर deposit होगी, और black tar में thermally decompose होकर jet nozzles block कर देगी। Recovery के लिए complete pump disassembly और cleaning चाहिए — days of downtime।

2. Top-up करते वक्त old oil में new oil mix करना। Oil level थोड़ा drop होता है — instinct है full change की जगह fresh oil top up करो। अगर existing oil clean है तो OK है। लेकिन अगर oil already degraded (yellow-brown) है, तो fresh oil ऊपर डालने से वो restore नहीं होती — degraded oil, new oil को contaminate कर देती है। अगर attention चाहिए तो पहले completely drain करें।

3. Roughing pump ने rough vacuum achieve करने से पहले diffusion pump heater start करना। Diffusion pump को तभी heat और run करें जब rotary pump पहले से ~0.5 Torr से कम rough vacuum achieve कर चुका हो। अगर diffusion pump atmospheric या near-atmospheric pressure पर start हो तो hot silicone oil oxygen से expose होकर rapidly oxidise होगी — एक ही run में dark brown या black हो जाएगी। ये diffusion pump oil को destroy करने का सबसे fast तरीका है और purely procedural error है।

Maintenance team के साथ share करें। ये तीन mistakes plants को pump repairs में हज़ारों और days of downtime खर्च कराती हैं — सब preventable हैं।

💡 Key Takeaway: Silicone diffusion pump oil — Bobst में CVC-4, Dow Corning में DC-704 (discontinued), Supervac India में SV-Sigma, दूसरों में Hivac F-4 / Indomet Tetra — सब same chemistry हैं। ये एक high-purity tetramethyltetraphenyl trisiloxane fluid है जो diffusion pump में continuous vaporise-jet-condense cycle से deep vacuum create करती है। ये lubricant नहीं है — ये खुद pumping mechanism है। आपके metalliser में तीन completely अलग pumps में तीन अलग oils हैं — rotary pump oil, booster pump oil, और diffusion pump oil — इन्हें mix करने से serious damage होता है। Diffusion pump oil तब बदलें जब वो brown या black हो, vacuum ज़्यादा time ले, या ultimate vacuum worse हो जाए — fixed calendar पर नहीं। तीन सबसे damaging mistakes: wrong oil डालना, degraded oil में fresh oil mix करना without draining, और rough vacuum achieve करने से पहले diffusion pump heater start करना।
