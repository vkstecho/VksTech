🔬 Why Aluminium for Metallisation? The 7 Metals We Could Use — And Why We Don't

Walk into any flexible packaging plant and ask why we metallise with aluminium. Most operators will say "because that's what we've always used." Most engineers will say "because it's cheap." Both answers are incomplete. The real answer is a 90-year story of physics, chemistry, economics, and food safety — and the result is one of the most carefully optimised material choices in modern manufacturing. Aluminium is not the cheapest metal, not the shiniest, not the most conductive. But across the 7 metrics that matter for packaging, no other metal comes close.

This blog breaks down why aluminium is the overwhelmingly dominant choice for metallised packaging globally — from chips packets to pharmaceutical blisters to cigarette foils. We will compare aluminium against 7 alternative metals that theoretically could replace it, explain the physics of why each alternative fails, and show where specialty metals like silver and copper DO win in niche applications. All reference prices are from April 2026 market data (LME and Indian bullion benchmarks) and are date-stamped accordingly. These absolute prices fluctuate daily — so we also show the cost ratios vs aluminium, which stay broadly stable over decades. If you have ever wondered what makes aluminium special, or why your customer specifies aluminium only, this is the complete answer.

⚙️ How Vacuum Metallisation Actually Works

Before comparing metals, understand the process constraint. Vacuum metallisation is not electroplating. In a metalliser machine, the film runs over a chilled drum inside a vacuum chamber. Below the drum, ceramic boats glow red-hot (around 1400-1600°C), and solid metal wire is fed into these boats. The metal melts, then vaporises, and the metal vapour travels upward to coat the moving film.

This process places a hard physics constraint on which metals can even be candidates: the metal must vaporise at a temperature the equipment can handle. Metals with very high boiling points (tungsten at 5,555°C, carbon at 4,827°C) simply cannot be evaporated with industrial equipment. This alone eliminates most of the periodic table before we even discuss cost.

The metallisation process happens in 5 distinct stages, each critical for quality output. A single interruption at any stage can compromise the entire batch of film. This is why metalliser operators follow strict SOPs for every shift.

Understanding these 5 stages is important because it reveals another reason aluminium dominates: the entire process is optimised for aluminium's specific properties. Ceramic evaporation boats, wire feed systems, vacuum levels, and drum temperatures — all designed around aluminium. Switching to a different metal would require rebuilding the entire equipment ecosystem.

These ratios tell the full economic story. For a mass-market chip packet costing ₹10 to the consumer, even the tiny amount of metal needed for metallisation has to fit within a few paise of raw material budget. The insight: The exact per-packet rupee cost is less important than the ratio. Silver being ~680× more expensive than aluminium means a plant switching from aluminium to silver would see its metal raw material cost rise by roughly 68,000%. No FMCG company can absorb that cost for zero measurable barrier improvement. Economics, not physics, is why aluminium won — and why aluminium will keep winning.

🔬 The Physics of "Good Enough" — Why Aluminium's Barrier is Already Maximum

Here is the beautiful technical insight that explains everything: once a metal layer is 40 nanometres thick or more, it is essentially opaque to light, oxygen, and moisture. Doubling the thickness does not double the barrier. Using a more premium metal does not improve the barrier. The barrier is already at its physical maximum.

This means:

  • Aluminium at 40nm blocks 99.9% of oxygen, light, and moisture
  • Silver at 40nm blocks 99.9% of oxygen, light, and moisture
  • Gold at 40nm blocks 99.9% of oxygen, light, and moisture

The barrier quality of aluminium is physically indistinguishable from silver or gold at the same thickness. So why pay hundreds of times more for silver, or tens of thousands of times more for gold? You only pay extra if you need an aesthetic quality (like a metallic gold shimmer for perfume packaging), not for barrier performance.

🌍 The Sustainability Angle — Why Aluminium Will Stay Dominant

Modern packaging must answer one more question: what happens after use? Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) regulations in India, EU, and emerging markets increasingly penalise unrecyclable packaging. This is where aluminium's position is actually strengthening:

  • Aluminium is infinitely recyclable — it can be melted and reused without property loss
  • Aluminium recycling uses 95% less energy than producing virgin aluminium
  • The metallised layer is so thin (typically 20-40nm) that it contributes a very small fraction of total packet weight
  • Plastic-aluminium separation technology is maturing rapidly in recycling facilities

Compare this to emerging metallisation alternatives like silicon oxide (SiOx) — which offer similar barrier but require more expensive processes. Aluminium's combination of recyclability, low energy footprint, and established infrastructure gives it a sustainability advantage that is difficult to displace.

🎓 Common Questions from Engineers and Students

Q1: Why is the aluminium layer so thin (40nm)? Can't we make it thicker for better barrier?

The 40nm thickness is already at the point of diminishing returns. Going thicker wastes aluminium (cost), reduces flexibility (film may crack at bends), and increases environmental burden. 40nm is the engineering sweet spot.

Q2: What is the difference between metallised film and aluminium foil?

Aluminium foil is typically 9-20 micrometres (9,000-20,000 nanometres) thick — about 500× thicker than metallised film. Foil is self-supporting metal; metallised film is a plastic substrate with a vapour-deposited metal layer. Foil is used for yoghurt lids, confectionery; metallised film is used for chips, biscuits, laminate pouches.

Q3: Why do some plants use two-layer aluminium (double metallised)?

For specialty high-barrier applications (pharma, ultra-long shelf life products), a second metallisation pass adds redundancy against pinholes. Pinholes are microscopic defects in the metal layer. A second layer covers the first layer's pinholes, reducing the effective pinhole density from 1 per cm² to near zero.

Q4: Is aluminium safe for direct food contact?

Yes, when used within regulatory specifications. The aluminium layer is typically NOT in direct food contact — it is laminated between plastic layers. Modern packaging uses the structure [outer printable layer]-[aluminium]-[inner sealant layer]. The sealant layer (PE, PP) is what touches the food.

🛡️ Quick Reference — When to Specify Which Metal

  • Default choice for standard flexible packaging: Aluminium
  • Transparent packaging with barrier: Aluminium Oxide (AlOx) or Silicon Oxide (SiOx)
  • Premium visual/luxury: Gold (cosmetic), Silver (premium food)
  • Antimicrobial requirement: Silver (medical, specialty food)
  • Non-food decorative: Copper, mixed metallic inks
  • Electronic/industrial: Chromium, Nickel (shielding applications)
  • Retort pouches visible food: AlOx for transparency + barrier

⚙️ Vacuum Metallisation actually कैसे काम करता है

Metals compare करने से पहले, process की constraint समझो। Vacuum metallisation electroplating नहीं है। Metalliser machine में film vacuum chamber के अंदर एक chilled drum पे चलती है। Drum के नीचे ceramic boats lal-गरम होते हैं (लगभग 1400-1600°C) और solid metal wire इन boats में feed होती है। Metal पिघलता है, फिर vaporise होता है, और metal vapour ऊपर जाकर चलती film को coat करता है।

इस process की एक hard physics constraint है कि कौन सा metal candidate भी बन सकता है: Metal का vaporisation temperature equipment handle कर पाए। बहुत high boiling point वाले metals (Tungsten 5,555°C, Carbon 4,827°C) industrial equipment से evaporate ही नहीं हो सकते। Cost की बात करने से पहले ही इससे periodic table के ज़्यादातर metals बाहर हो जाते हैं।

Metallisation process 5 अलग stages में होती है, हर stage quality output के लिए critical है। किसी भी stage में एक interruption पूरे batch की quality compromise कर सकता है। इसीलिए metalliser operators हर shift में strict SOPs follow करते हैं।

ये 5 stages समझना इसलिए important है क्योंकि ये एक और कारण बताते हैं कि क्यों aluminium dominate करता है: पूरी process aluminium की specific properties के लिए optimised है। Ceramic evaporation boats, wire feed systems, vacuum levels, और drum temperatures — सब aluminium के लिए designed हैं। दूसरे metal पर switch करना पूरा equipment ecosystem फिर से बनाने जैसा होगा।

🔬 "Good Enough" की Physics — Aluminium का Barrier पहले से Maximum क्यों है

ये beautiful technical insight है जो सब कुछ explain करता है: एक metal layer जब 40 nanometres या उससे ज़्यादा मोटी हो जाती है, तब वो practically light, oxygen, moisture के लिए opaque हो जाती है। Thickness double करने से barrier double नहीं होता। Premium metal use करने से barrier नहीं सुधरता। Barrier पहले से ही physical maximum पे है।

मतलब:

  • 40nm पर Aluminium 99.9% oxygen, light, moisture block करता है
  • 40nm पर Silver 99.9% oxygen, light, moisture block करता है
  • 40nm पर Gold 99.9% oxygen, light, moisture block करता है

Aluminium की barrier quality same thickness पर silver या gold से physically indistinguishable है। तो silver के लिए सैकड़ों गुना, और gold के लिए हज़ारों गुना ज़्यादा क्यों pay करें? Extra तभी pay करते हो जब aesthetic quality चाहिए हो (जैसे perfume packaging पर metallic gold shimmer) — barrier performance के लिए नहीं।

AlOx का case specially interesting है। जब customer transparent packaging चाहे (जैसे ready-to-eat meals के clear retort pouch) — pure aluminium नहीं चलेगा, वो opaque silver है। Solution है aluminium oxide — जो 90% aluminium का barrier देता है और transparent होता है। इसी कारण AlOx premium flexible packaging में बढ़ता niche बन गया है।

🌍 Sustainability Angle — Aluminium Dominant क्यों रहेगा

Modern packaging को एक और सवाल का जवाब देना पड़ता है: use के बाद क्या? India, EU, और emerging markets की Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) regulations unrecyclable packaging पर लगातार penalty बढ़ा रही हैं। यहाँ aluminium की position actually मज़बूत हो रही है:

  • Aluminium infinitely recyclable है — पिघला के बिना property loss के फिर use हो जाता है
  • Aluminium recycling में 95% कम energy लगती है — virgin aluminium के मुक़ाबले
  • Metallised layer इतनी पतली है (typically 20-40nm) कि total packet weight में बहुत छोटा fraction contribute करती है
  • Plastic-aluminium separation technology recycling facilities में तेज़ी से mature हो रही है

इसकी तुलना emerging alternatives जैसे silicon oxide (SiOx) से करो — ये समान barrier देते हैं लेकिन ज़्यादा महँगे processes से। Aluminium का recyclability, कम energy footprint, और established infrastructure का combination — ऐसा sustainability advantage है जो displace करना बहुत मुश्किल है।

🎓 Engineers और Students के आम सवाल

Q1: Aluminium layer इतनी पतली (40nm) क्यों है? Better barrier के लिए thicker नहीं बना सकते?

40nm thickness पहले से diminishing returns के point पे है। ज़्यादा thick करने से aluminium waste (cost), flexibility कम (film bend पर crack कर सकती है), और environmental burden बढ़ता है। 40nm engineering का sweet spot है।

Q2: Metallised film और Aluminium foil में फ़र्क क्या है?

Aluminium foil typically 9-20 micrometres (9,000-20,000 nanometres) मोटा होता है — metallised film से लगभग 500× ज़्यादा। Foil self-supporting metal है; metallised film plastic substrate है जिस पे vapour-deposited metal layer है। Foil use होता है yoghurt lids, confectionery में; metallised film use होता है chips, biscuits, laminate pouches में।

Q3: कुछ plants two-layer aluminium (double metallised) क्यों use करते हैं?

Specialty high-barrier applications (pharma, ultra-long shelf life) के लिए दूसरा metallisation pass pinholes के against redundancy देता है। Pinholes metal layer में microscopic defects हैं। दूसरी layer पहली layer के pinholes cover करती है — effective pinhole density 1 per cm² से लगभग zero तक।

Q4: क्या Aluminium direct food contact के लिए safe है?

हाँ, regulatory specifications के अंदर use हो तो। Aluminium layer typically food के direct contact में नहीं होती — plastic layers के बीच laminate होती है। Modern packaging structure: [outer printable layer]-[aluminium]-[inner sealant layer]। Sealant layer (PE, PP) food को touch करती है।

🛡️ Quick Reference — कब कौन सा Metal Specify करें

  • Standard Flexible packaging का default choice: Aluminium
  • Barrier के साथ Transparent packaging: Aluminium Oxide (AlOx) या Silicon Oxide (SiOx)
  • Premium visual/luxury: Gold (cosmetic), Silver (premium food)
  • Antimicrobial requirement: Silver (medical, specialty food)
  • Non-food decorative: Copper, mixed metallic inks
  • Electronic/industrial: Chromium, Nickel (shielding applications)
  • Visible food वाले retort pouches: AlOx — transparency + barrier
