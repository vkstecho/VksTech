🛡️ OTR & WVTR — The Two Numbers That Decide What Your Package Can Hold

📖 14-min read  |  The Barrier Property Bible for Flexible Packaging Engineers  |  Published May 2026  |  Includes dual-mode calculator + bookmark-able cheat sheet

Every flexible packaging conversation eventually arrives at the same two acronyms: OTR and WVTR. Customer asks for a barrier spec — they're really asking for OTR and WVTR. Procurement compares two suppliers' films — they're comparing OTR and WVTR. R&D evaluates a new substrate — they measure OTR and WVTR. Yet many operators on the shop floor have only the vaguest sense of what these numbers mean, what units they come in, and how to translate "I need to package this product for 12 months" into "the laminate needs OTR <1 and WVTR <1." This blog closes that gap. By the end you will know exactly what OTR and WVTR are, how they're measured, what values different substrates deliver, what values different food products require, and — using the calculator at the bottom — which structure to specify for any product. This is the page to bookmark and the cheat sheet to print.

📌 Quick Reference — The Two Numbers in 30 Seconds Skip to Calculator →
OTR — OXYGEN TRANSMISSION RATE
How much oxygen passes through 1 m² of film per day. Measured at 23°C, 0% RH, in cc/m²/day (cubic centimetres of O₂). Lower = better. Bare PET ≈ 110. Metallised PET <1. Aluminium foil ≈ 0.
WVTR — WATER VAPOUR TRANSMISSION RATE
How much water vapour passes through 1 m² of film per day. Measured at 38°C, 90% RH, in g/m²/day (grams of H₂O). Lower = better. Bare PET ≈ 40. Metallised PET <1. Aluminium foil ≈ 0.
THE PRODUCT-DRIVEN RULE
Coffee/pharma needs OTR <0.5, WVTR <1 → metallised structures. Snacks need OTR <5, WVTR <2 → metallised. Atta needs OTR <50, WVTR <3 → BOPP+PE laminate, no metallisation. Bulk grains need OTR <200 → simple LDPE bag. Match the barrier to the product, not the other way around.

🧪 What These Numbers Actually Mean

A package is a slow leak. Everything leaks — the question is how slowly, and whether the leak rate is slow enough to keep the product inside fresh until the consumer opens it. OTR and WVTR are the two numbers that quantify this leak rate for the two molecules that matter most in food preservation: oxygen (causes oxidation, rancidity in fats, aroma loss in coffee, fat bloom in chocolate) and water vapour (causes loss of crispness in chips, mold growth on bread, caking in spices, stickiness in toffee).

OTR — Oxygen Transmission Rate. The volume of oxygen, measured in cubic centimetres at standard temperature and pressure, that passes through 1 square metre of the packaging film in 24 hours, when the film is held at 23°C and 0% relative humidity, with one side exposed to pure oxygen and the other to a stream of inert gas. Standard unit: cc/m²/day. Sometimes written as cm³/m²/day or with the suffix "(STP)" for "standard temperature and pressure." Measured on a Mocon Ox-Tran or equivalent.

WVTR — Water Vapour Transmission Rate. The mass of water vapour, measured in grams, that passes through 1 square metre of the packaging film in 24 hours, when the film is held at 38°C (≈100°F) and 90% relative humidity, with one side exposed to high-humidity air and the other to dry desiccant. Standard unit: g/m²/day. Measured on a Mocon Permatran or by gravimetric cup methods (ASTM E96).

Both numbers are properties of the laminate as a whole, not just one layer. A 12-micron PET film bonded to a 50-micron LDPE sealant has a different OTR/WVTR than the PET alone — usually slightly worse, because the LDPE adds its own transmission to the path. The lowest-barrier layer in the structure dominates. If your beautiful Met PET has a microscopic pinhole, the entire laminate's barrier collapses to whatever the LDPE alone can provide.

📏 Why the Test Conditions Matter

The 23°C/0% RH for OTR and 38°C/90% RH for WVTR are not arbitrary. Both standards were chosen to represent realistic worst-case storage conditions for tropical markets and to give comparable numbers across labs and suppliers. But test conditions matter enormously for some materials:

MaterialOTR @ 50% RHOTR @ 90% RHSensitivity
PET (bare)~110~115Low — PET is humidity-stable
BOPP (bare)~2,400~2,500Low
Metallised PET~0.5~0.7Slight — Al layer is humidity-stable
EVOH~0.1~0.3-1.0Severe — humidity tripled OTR or more
Nylon (BOPA)~40~80-100Severe — humidity doubles OTR

This is why a single OTR number on a spec sheet can mislead you. EVOH is the world's best oxygen barrier at 0% RH, and it's a mediocre one at 90% RH. If you're packaging a product for the Indian monsoon market, you cannot use the dry-condition OTR as your spec — you need the high-humidity number. Always ask your supplier for the test conditions, and always ask for the value at the humidity your product will actually see.

🔬 How They're Measured

Two main methods dominate industrial barrier testing: coulometric for OTR (Mocon Ox-Tran) and infrared sensor or gravimetric for WVTR (Mocon Permatran or cup method). The principle is the same in all cases: clamp a film sample in a chamber, expose one side to the test gas (O₂ or H₂O), put a sensor or absorbent on the other side, measure how much gas appears on the sensor side per unit time per unit area. The math gives you cc/m²/day or g/m²/day.

Most Indian lab houses charge ₹3,000-8,000 per OTR/WVTR test pair, with results in 5-10 days. SGS, Bureau Veritas, and ICMR-affiliated labs are the most-cited sources. For high-volume production, in-house Mocon machines are the norm at large converters — the capital cost runs ₹40-60 lakh per machine, but the running cost per test drops below ₹500.

One important caveat: barrier numbers from sales literature are usually "best-case lab conditions" — pristine film, no creases, no flex damage, optimal temperature control. Real-world flex-converted, printed, laminated, slit, rewound film typically delivers 1.5× to 3× higher OTR/WVTR than the lab number, because of pinholes from flex cracking, solvent retention from printing, and lamination defects. Always design with a 2× safety margin against the spec sheet number.

⚠️ Six Common Spec Mistakes That Cost Real Money

Specifying barrier is one of those engineering tasks where small mistakes have big downstream consequences. The six errors below are responsible for the majority of barrier-related quality issues at flexible packaging plants:

1. Confusing units between standards. ASTM (American) tests OTR at 23°C/0% RH. JIS (Japanese) often uses 23°C/65% RH. DIN (European) sometimes uses 25°C/85% RH. The same film can show OTR values 10-30% apart depending on which standard. Always ask which standard your supplier used.

2. Quoting individual layer values for the laminate. A 3-layer laminate of PET (OTR 110) + Met PET (OTR 0.5) + LDPE (OTR 7800) has a laminate OTR closer to the Met PET value (~0.5-0.7), not the average of the layers. The lowest-barrier layer dominates — but pinholes in that layer can collapse the whole structure to the worst-barrier layer's value.

3. Forgetting humidity dependence. EVOH OTR can triple between dry and humid conditions. Nylon doubles. If your product ships through monsoon humidity, the dry-state spec is meaningless. Test at the humidity the product will actually see.

4. Over-specifying barrier. Specifying Met PET when Bare PET would do is the most common cost error in the Indian market. A 30g pouch of atta does not need OTR <1 — it needs OTR <50. The cost difference between Met PET-LDPE and BOPP-LDPE structures is ~30%. Across millions of pouches, that's lakhs per month wasted.

5. Confusing barrier with active oxygen scavenger. A barrier slows oxygen transmission from outside in. An oxygen scavenger (typically iron-based sachets or oxygen-absorbing polymers in the structure) removes oxygen already inside the package. They solve different problems. For long-shelf-life products like premium coffee, you typically need both.

6. Specifying barrier without product testing. The gold standard is Accelerated Shelf-Life Testing (ASLT) — pack the product in candidate structures, store at elevated temperature (40°C, 75% RH typical) for 4-12 weeks, then evaluate sensory and microbial parameters. This catches issues that pure barrier numbers miss: sealing defects, migration, off-flavour development. Spec barrier first, ASLT to confirm.

💡 The Engineering Mindset

The right way to use OTR and WVTR is not to memorise specific values but to develop intuition for orders of magnitude. Numbers under 1 mean foil-class barrier — that's metallisation territory, expensive, premium products only. Numbers in the 1-10 range mean snack-class barrier — typical metallised structures. Numbers in the 10-100 range mean atta/dal-class barrier — bare laminates do the job. Numbers above 100 mean the barrier is essentially mechanical only — bulk grains, sugar, anything where the product itself isn't sensitive.

Once you have that intuition, the conversation with customers and procurement gets a lot more productive. Instead of "what barrier does the customer want?" you can ask "what shelf life does the product need, and at what storage conditions?" — and translate that into the OTR/WVTR target yourself. That's the engineer's value-add. The calculator above is a starting point; the intuition is what makes you indispensable.

🧪 ये Numbers Actually क्या मतलब रखते हैं

Package एक slow leak है। सब कुछ leak करता है — सवाल ये है कितना slowly, और क्या leak rate इतना slow है कि product अंदर fresh रहे जब तक consumer खोले। OTR और WVTR वो दो numbers हैं जो उन दो molecules के लिए ये leak rate quantify करते हैं जो food preservation में सबसे ज़्यादा matter करते हैं: oxygen (oxidation cause करता है, fats में rancidity, coffee में aroma loss, chocolate में fat bloom) और water vapour (chips में crispness loss, bread पर mold growth, spices में caking, toffee में stickiness)।

OTR — Oxygen Transmission Rate। Oxygen का volume, standard temperature और pressure पर cubic centimetres में measured, जो 1 square metre packaging film से 24 hours में pass होता है, जब film 23°C और 0% relative humidity पर hold हो, एक side pure oxygen exposed और दूसरी inert gas के stream को। Standard unit: cc/m²/day। कभी-कभी cm³/m²/day लिखा जाता है या "(STP)" suffix के साथ "standard temperature and pressure" के लिए। Mocon Ox-Tran या equivalent पर measure।

WVTR — Water Vapour Transmission Rate। Water vapour का mass, grams में measured, जो 1 square metre packaging film से 24 hours में pass होता है, जब film 38°C (≈100°F) और 90% relative humidity पर hold हो, एक side high-humidity air exposed और दूसरी dry desiccant। Standard unit: g/m²/day। Mocon Permatran पर या gravimetric cup methods (ASTM E96) से measure।

दोनों numbers laminate के whole की properties हैं, सिर्फ़ एक layer की नहीं। 12-micron PET film जो 50-micron LDPE sealant से bonded है उसका OTR/WVTR PET अकेले से अलग होगा — usually slightly worse, क्योंकि LDPE अपनी transmission path में add करती है। Structure की lowest-barrier layer dominate करती है। अगर आपकी beautiful Met PET में microscopic pinhole है, पूरी laminate का barrier जो भी LDPE अकेले provide कर सके उसी पर collapse हो जाता है।

🔬 कैसे Measure होते हैं

Industrial barrier testing में दो main methods dominate करते हैं: coulometric OTR के लिए (Mocon Ox-Tran) और infrared sensor या gravimetric WVTR के लिए (Mocon Permatran या cup method)। Principle सब cases में same है: chamber में film sample clamp करो, एक side test gas (O₂ या H₂O) expose करो, दूसरी पर sensor या absorbent रखो, measure करो कितना gas sensor side पर per unit time per unit area आता है। Math आपको cc/m²/day या g/m²/day देता है।

Indian lab houses ज़्यादातर ₹3,000-8,000 per OTR/WVTR test pair charge करते हैं, results 5-10 दिन में। SGS, Bureau Veritas, और ICMR-affiliated labs सबसे ज़्यादा cited sources हैं। High-volume production के लिए, बड़े converters के पास in-house Mocon machines norm हैं — capital cost ₹40-60 lakh per machine, पर per-test running cost ₹500 से नीचे drop होती है।

⚠️ Real Money Cost करने वाली छह Common Spec Mistakes

Barrier specify करना उन engineering tasks में से एक है जहाँ छोटी mistakes बड़े downstream consequences रखती हैं। नीचे की छह errors flexible packaging plants पर majority barrier-related quality issues के लिए responsible हैं:

1. Standards के बीच units confuse करना। ASTM (American) OTR 23°C/0% RH पर test करता है। JIS (Japanese) अक्सर 23°C/65% RH use करता है। DIN (European) कभी 25°C/85% RH। Same film 10-30% apart OTR values show कर सकती है depending on standard। हमेशा supplier से पूछें कौन सा standard use किया।

2. Laminate के लिए individual layer values quote करना। 3-layer laminate of PET (OTR 110) + Met PET (OTR 0.5) + LDPE (OTR 7800) का laminate OTR Met PET value (~0.5-0.7) के closer है, layers का average नहीं। Lowest-barrier layer dominate करती है — पर उस layer में pinholes whole structure को worst-barrier layer की value पर collapse कर सकते हैं।

3. Humidity dependence भूलना। EVOH OTR dry और humid conditions के बीच triple हो सकती है। Nylon double हो जाती है। अगर आपका product monsoon humidity ship होता है, dry-state spec meaningless है। Product जिस humidity देखेगा उस पर test करें।

4. Barrier over-specify करना। Met PET specify करना जब Bare PET काम करेगी Indian market में सबसे common cost error है। 30g pouch of आटा को OTR <1 नहीं चाहिए — OTR <50 चाहिए। Met PET-LDPE और BOPP-LDPE structures के बीच cost difference ~30% है। Millions of pouches पर, वो lakhs per month wasted।

5. Barrier और active oxygen scavenger confuse करना। Barrier oxygen transmission outside से inside slow करता है। Oxygen scavenger (typically iron-based sachets या oxygen-absorbing polymers structure में) oxygen को remove करता है जो already inside package है। वो different problems solve करते हैं। Long-shelf-life products जैसे premium coffee के लिए, typically दोनों चाहिए।

6. Product testing के बिना barrier specify करना। Gold standard है Accelerated Shelf-Life Testing (ASLT) — product को candidate structures में pack करो, elevated temperature (40°C, 75% RH typical) पर 4-12 weeks store करो, फिर sensory और microbial parameters evaluate करो। ये उन issues को catch करता है जो pure barrier numbers miss करते हैं: sealing defects, migration, off-flavour development। Barrier पहले spec करो, ASLT confirm करने के लिए।

💡 Engineering Mindset

OTR और WVTR use करने का सही way specific values memorise करना नहीं है पर orders of magnitude के लिए intuition develop करना है। 1 के नीचे numbers मतलब foil-class barrier — वो metallisation territory, expensive, premium products only। 1-10 range के numbers मतलब snack-class barrier — typical metallised structures। 10-100 range मतलब atta/dal-class barrier — bare laminates काम करते हैं। 100 के ऊपर मतलब barrier essentially mechanical only — bulk grains, sugar, कुछ भी जहाँ product itself sensitive नहीं।

एक बार वो intuition आ गई, customers और procurement के साथ conversation बहुत productive हो जाती है। "Customer क्या barrier चाहता है?" के बजाय आप पूछ सकते हैं "product को क्या shelf life चाहिए, और क्या storage conditions पर?" — और उसे OTR/WVTR target में खुद translate कर सकते हैं। वो engineer का value-add है। ऊपर का calculator starting point है; intuction वो है जो आपको indispensable बनाती है।

💡 Key Takeaway: OTR (oxygen) और WVTR (water vapour) transmission rates वो दो numbers हैं जो decide करते हैं हर flexible package क्या hold कर सकता है। OTR units: cc/m²/day at 23°C, 0% RH। WVTR units: g/m²/day at 38°C, 90% RH। Lower = better barrier। Bare PET ≈ 110 OTR / 40 WVTR; Metallised PET <1 / <1; Aluminium foil ≈ 0/0। Product की needs को substrate की capability से match करें: coffee/pharma को <0.5/<1 चाहिए (metallised structures); snacks को <5/<2 (metallised); atta को <50/<3 (BOPP+PE laminate); bulk grains को <200 (LDPE bag)। Common mistakes: ASTM/DIN/JIS standards के बीच units confuse करना, laminates के लिए layer values quote करना, humidity dependence भूलना (EVOH monsoon में triple), barrier over-specify करना (Met PET जब Bare PET काम करेगी), और passive barrier को active oxygen scavengers से confuse करना। हमेशा spec sheet values के against 2× safety margin design करें, और Accelerated Shelf-Life Testing से confirm करें।
