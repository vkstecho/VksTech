🔑 Wrinkle vs Crease — The 2-Second Shop Floor Test

When you spot a fold on the slitter, perform this test immediately: stretch the film flat by hand. If the fold disappears, it is a wrinkle. If the fold remains visible, it is a crease.

DefectWhat You ObserveTest ResultCan You Save It?
WrinkleTemporary fold that appears and disappears during the runDisappears when stretchedYes — fix the source and re-run
CreasePermanent line pressed in by a nip or lay-on rollerRemains visible when stretchedNo — downgrade to B-Grade or scrap

The golden rule: every crease began as a wrinkle somewhere upstream. If you eliminate wrinkles at the source, creases never form. Crease repair does not exist as a process — only wrinkle prevention works. This is why the detection method in this blog focuses entirely on finding and fixing wrinkles before they become creases.

⚠️ Why This Defect Is So Expensive

  • Customer laminators stop running: creased metallised film breaks on their production line, and the entire consignment gets rejected
  • B-Grade penalty of 30-40%: even visible wrinkles that never became creases still downgrade the roll, reducing realised price significantly
  • Rework is physically impossible: the aluminium coating and base film are permanently damaged — unlike OD problems, you cannot reprocess
  • Reputation damage spreads: one customer complaint about wrinkles becomes a narrative that affects future orders from the same account
  • Downstream slitter damage: creased web causes blade chatter, uneven winding tension, and telescoping on the output roll

🔍 The 5 Wrinkle Patterns — Each Pattern Tells a Different Story

Every wrinkle falls into one of five patterns. Recognising the pattern on sight is the most valuable diagnostic skill an operator can develop, because each pattern points to a specific category of cause.

▌▌▌ Pattern 1 — Tin-Canning (MD Symmetric)

Parallel vertical lines at uniform spacing, resembling the ribs of a tin roof

➤ Cause: The web has expanded from tension drop, heat, or humidity and has excess width with nowhere to go.
➤ Fix: Keep tension constant across zones. Install a bowed spreader roller or a larger-diameter idler with shallow grooves.

▌ ▌ Pattern 2 — Single Bulge (MD Asymmetric)

Multiple vertical wrinkles collecting into one prominent bulge or crease somewhere across the width

➤ Cause: Gauge band or baggy area in the unwind roll, spreader misalignment, or uneven tension distribution across the web width.
➤ Fix: Check the unwind roll with a micrometer. If gauge variation exceeds ±3%, raise an NCR with the supplier. Verify spreader alignment.

═══ Pattern 3 — Cross-Direction Lines

Horizontal stripes running perpendicular to web travel

➤ Cause: Heated roller running too hot, excess nip pressure, or tension pulses from a worn gearbox or drive belt.
➤ Fix: Reduce heated roller temperature by 5-10°C. Verify nip pressure against specification. Inspect the drive train for worn bearings.

↗↗↗ Pattern 4 — Bow Wrinkle (Diagonal Symmetric)

Chevron or arrowhead pattern pointing downstream, starting from both edges and fading toward the centre

➤ Cause: A smile-shaped roller — curved from excessive deflection, typically because its diameter is too small for the web width and tension load.
➤ Fix: Replace with a larger-diameter roller. If it is a nip roll, verify the crown matches the current tension load.

↗ Pattern 5 — One-Sided Diagonal (Diagonal Asymmetric)

Diagonal fold from one edge only, often accompanied by web tracking toward that side

➤ Cause: Misaligned roller with one end higher or rotated, a worn bearing on one side only, or a tilted machine frame.
➤ Fix: Use a laser alignment tool on all rollers upstream from the defect point. Replace worn bearings. Verify frame leveling.

🏭 Real Shop Floor Story — The Bearing Check That Saved ₹8 Lakh

Night shift, 2 AM. Met Slitter-1. Every 12-micron metallised PET roll coming off the machine showed vertical wrinkles in the classic tin-canning pattern. The operator called the shift supervisor.

"Sir — tension up or down?"

The supervisor adjusted tension three times. No improvement. Two more rolls went to B-Grade. By the morning meeting, the blame sequence had already begun.

Week 1: The film supplier was blamed — certificates came back showing the jumbos were within specification.
Week 2: The anti-static bar was serviced — the wrinkles continued.
Week 3: Nip pressure was reduced — the web began to slip, and the wrinkles worsened.
Week 4: Monthly loss reached ₹8 lakh in B-Grade downgrades and wasted film.

Then a senior technical consultant arrived for a routine audit and was told about the problem. His approach was different.

"Don't touch anything. Walk with me."

He started at the rewinder and walked backwards along the web path, holding a portable lamp at a low angle. At idler roller number six — five stations upstream of the rewinder — he stopped.

"Look at the shadow here. The wrinkle is forming at this roller."

Idler number six was pulled during the weekend shutdown. The bearing on the operator side had 2mm of radial play. Under load, the roller was running slightly off-axis, forming a subtle smile shape that the web had to conform to. It was not bad enough to create noise. Not enough to show up on a routine PM check. But it was more than enough to create perfect conditions for MD wrinkle formation on every pass.

Total fix cost: ₹3,800 for a new bearing plus 4 hours of labour, totalling ₹6,500. Monthly saving: ₹8 lakh. Payback: 4 hours. The wrinkles disappeared completely in the very first shift after the bearing change. That engineer now runs a monthly laser alignment check on all slitter rollers — a 90-minute job that prevents months of downgrades.

💡 The Lesson — Alignment-First, Not Tension-First. Every time tension is adjusted without first walking the line, the symptom gets treated while the root cause hides. Plants that eliminate wrinkle problems permanently are the plants that stop at the first roller where a shadow appears — not the last one where the fold becomes visible.

🛡️ Prevention — 8 Routines That Stop Wrinkles Before They Start

  • Weekly laser alignment check on every idler, nip roll, and spreader. Thirty minutes a week against ₹8 lakh a month in downgrades is the right trade
  • Bearing play check every PM cycle. Push each roller laterally by hand. Any perceivable play means the bearing needs replacement — no exceptions
  • Taper tension enabled at the rewinder. As diameter grows, tension must reduce proportionally. Constant tension from core to full roll guarantees wrinkles near the end
  • IPA wipe on all idlers every shift. Surface deposits create local tension differences that become MD wrinkles within a few jumbos
  • Foam end caps during every roll handling operation. A single forklift knock creates edge damage that propagates into CD wrinkles across the entire roll
  • Storage only in vertical orientation, never stacked horizontally. The weight of upper rolls creates permanent pressure creases on lower rolls
  • Heated roller temperature changes in 2°C steps. Sudden temperature swings cause the film to expand or contract faster than the web tension can accommodate
  • Strict FIFO with a 48-hour maximum handling window. Rolls sitting longer develop internal stress patterns that emerge as wrinkles during processing

🔍 5 Wrinkle Patterns — हर एक की अलग कहानी

हर wrinkle इन 5 patterns में से एक होती है। Pattern देखते ही cause समझ आ जाता है — ये operator का सबसे बड़ा skill है:

▌▌▌ Pattern 1 — खड़ी झुर्रियाँ (MD Symmetric / Tin-Canning)

Equal spacing में vertical parallel lines — tin की छत जैसी

➤ Cause: Web फूल गई — tension drop, heat, या humidity से। Extra width है, कहीं जाने की जगह नहीं।
➤ Fix: Zones में tension constant रखो। Bowed spreader roller लगाओ, या बड़ी diameter वाला idler with shallow grooves।

▌ ▌ Pattern 2 — इकट्ठा Bulge (MD Asymmetric)

खड़ी wrinkles एक जगह इकट्ठी हो कर bulge या crease बन जाती हैं

➤ Cause: Unwind roll में gauge band या baggy area। Spreader misalign। Width में tension uneven।
➤ Fix: Unwind roll micrometer से check करो। Gauge variation >±3% है? Supplier पे NCR उठाओ। Spreader alignment verify करो।

═══ Pattern 3 — पड़ी झुर्रियाँ (CD — Cross-Direction)

Machine direction के perpendicular — horizontal stripes

➤ Cause: Heated roller ज़्यादा गर्म। Nip pressure ज़्यादा। Worn gearbox या belt से tension में pulse।
➤ Fix: Heated roller temp 5-10°C कम करो। Nip pressure spec के against check। Drive train में worn bearings देखो।

↗↗↗ Pattern 4 — धनुष Pattern (Diagonal Symmetric / Bow)

Chevron या arrowhead — दोनों edges से शुरू हो कर center में मिलता है

➤ Cause: "Smile-shaped" roller — curved है। Deflection ज़्यादा क्योंकि diameter web width के हिसाब से छोटा है।
➤ Fix: बड़ी diameter वाला roller लगाओ। Nip roll है तो crown tension load से match करो।

↗ Pattern 5 — एक तरफ़ से (Diagonal Asymmetric)

सिर्फ़ एक edge से diagonal — web उस तरफ़ track कर रही है

➤ Cause: Roller misaligned — एक end ऊँची या rotated। एक side की bearing worn। Machine frame tilted।
➤ Fix: उस point से upstream सारे rollers laser align करो। Worn bearings replace। Frame leveling check।
