>
> **Background:** navy `#1A2744`
>
> **Header (top 10%, cream background strip):**
> - Eyebrow (orange): 6 WAYS YOUR BOAT FAILS — AND HOW TO PREVENT EVERY ONE
> - Title (navy bold): **Boat Failure Mode Reference Chart**
> - Subtitle (grey italic): *All 6 failures are preventable with correct procedure. Root cause + prevention for each.*
>
> **Main body (80%) — 2×3 GRID of failure cards:**
> Six equal-size cards (2 columns × 3 rows), each with:
> - A large failure number badge (orange circle, white bold number)
> - Failure name (white bold ~14pt)
> - Visual indicator (simple flat icon):
>   1. Boat with crack lines + steam bubble = **Thermal Shock Cracking**
>   2. Boat with wet left half / dry right half + crack line at boundary = **Dry/Wet Zone Cracking**
>   3. Boat with puncture hole + fire icon = **Puncture from Overheating**
>   4. Two copper clamps with pitted/rough surface + uneven current arrows = **Clamp Contact Failure**
>   5. Boat cavity with dark sludge build-up layer = **Sludge Build-up**
>   6. Boat in open chamber with O₂ molecules hitting its surface = **Air Exposure While Hot**
> - Root cause (teal text ~11pt): 1–2 lines
> - Prevention (orange text ~11pt): 1 line starting with ✓
>
> Card content:
>
> **Card 1 — Thermal Shock Cracking:**
> Root: *New boat powered up too fast. Moisture inside converts to steam, pressure cracks boat.*
> Prevention: ✓ *Ramp up slowly — 50–75% power for first 15–20 minutes*
>
> **Card 2 — Dry/Wet Zone Cracking:**
> Root: *Partial wetting only. Temperature difference between wet (cooled) and dry (hot) zones creates stress fractures.*
> Prevention: ✓ *Pre-wet cavity with U-shaped wire before closing chamber*
>
> **Card 3 — Puncture from Overheating:**
> Root: *Wire feed too slow for power level. Superheated Al corrodes through boat floor.*
> Prevention: ✓ *Always match wire feed rate to power. Never run power without wire.*
>
> **Card 4 — Clamp Contact Failure:**
> Root: *Rough, oxidised, or Al-contaminated clamps cause uneven current → hot spots at boat ends.*
> Prevention: ✓ *Clean clamp faces at every boat change. Replace worn clamps.*
>
> **Card 5 — Sludge Build-up:**
> Root: *AlN and oxidised Al compounds block cavity, reduce wetting area, create hot spots.*
> Prevention: ✓ *Scrape sludge with ceramic/wooden tool at every roll change*
>
> **Card 6 — Air Exposure While Hot:**
> Root: *Chamber vented while boats still at temperature. TiB₂ surface oxidises, wettability drops.*
> Prevention: ✓ *Wait 2 minutes after power off before venting. Boats must cool below red heat.*
>
> **Bottom strip (cream background, full width):**
> - LEFT (navy bold): ⚡ **THE MOST COMMON CAUSE IN INDIA:**
> - CENTRE (navy): *Thermal shock cracking on first use — caused by skipping the slow heat ramp. Takes 15 minutes to prevent. Costs one boat to skip.*
