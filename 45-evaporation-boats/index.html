WHAT IS IT?
An evaporation boat is a small rectangular ceramic component — roughly the size of a thick pencil eraser — that sits inside the vacuum chamber of a metalliser. It is connected to a high-current electrical supply. When current passes through it, the boat heats up to around 1400–1500°C. Aluminium wire is fed onto the hot boat, melts instantly, and evaporates — the vapour deposits on the film passing above as a thin metallic coating.
WHAT IS IT MADE OF?
TiB₂ + BN composite ceramic — Titanium Diboride (TiB₂) makes the boat electrically conductive so it can self-heat. Boron Nitride (BN) provides thermal shock resistance so it doesn't crack when heated and cooled hundreds of times. Together they create a material that can survive molten aluminium at 1400°C, cycle after cycle, for hundreds of hours — nothing else comes close.

🔌 How a Boat Works — Electricity, Heat, and Evaporation

Understanding why boats behave the way they do starts with understanding the basic physics. An evaporation boat is essentially a high-temperature resistor. Copper electrode clamps grip both ends of the boat and pass a high current — typically 400 to 900 amperes at 5 to 15 volts — through it. The boat's ceramic material has a carefully controlled electrical resistance. This resistance converts electrical energy into heat, just like a wire in an electric heater — except at a far more extreme scale. The entire boat reaches 1400–1500°C within seconds of full power being applied.

Current (400–900A) flows through the ceramic boat → heats to 1400–1500°C → aluminium wire melts on contact → evaporates → deposits on film as a mirror coating just a few hundred angstroms thick.

The aluminium wire — typically 1.5 to 2mm diameter — is fed from a spool at a controlled rate onto the centre cavity of the hot boat. The moment it touches the boat surface, it melts. The molten aluminium spreads across the boat's cavity surface — this spreading is called wetting — and the heat evaporates it into a cloud of aluminium vapour. That vapour travels upward in the vacuum and lands on the BOPET or BOPP film passing above the boat zone, forming the thin metallic coating you see on every shiny food pouch and snack packet.

A typical metalliser has many boats arranged in a row — commonly 10 to 20 boats side by side across the full width of the machine — so the entire film width gets coated uniformly in one pass. Each boat is powered independently through its own clamp pair, allowing operators to adjust power to individual boats to correct for uneven coating across the web width.

🧱 The Material — Why TiB₂ + BN and Nothing Else

The choice of TiB₂ (Titanium Diboride) and BN (Boron Nitride) as the ceramic composite for evaporation boats is not arbitrary. It is the result of decades of development that eliminated every other candidate material. Here is why this specific combination wins:

TiB₂ handles the electrical conductivity and high-temperature strength. BN handles the thermal shock resistance and chemical stability. Together they create a material that nothing else in industry can replicate for this application.

TiB₂ — Titanium Diboride (the conductor). TiB₂ is a hard, dense ceramic with a melting point of 2980°C — almost twice the operating temperature of the boat. It is electrically conductive, which is what allows the boat to be self-heated by passing current through it. Without TiB₂, the ceramic would be an insulator and could not be resistively heated at all. TiB₂ also provides hardness and structural strength to the boat body.

BN — Boron Nitride (the shock absorber). BN is a layered ceramic — hexagonal in structure, similar to graphite — that is electrically non-conductive but has exceptional thermal shock resistance. Every time a boat is heated up to 1400°C and then cooled back to room temperature (each metallising run), it experiences an enormous thermal cycle. Without BN's shock-absorbing properties, the boat would crack after just a few cycles. BN also acts as a lubricant at the microscopic level, allowing the thermal expansion and contraction without stress fractures propagating through the body.

The ratio matters. The ratio of TiB₂ to BN determines the boat's character. Higher TiB₂ content = better electrical conductivity, better wettability, but more susceptibility to corrosion by molten aluminium. Higher BN content = better thermal shock resistance, longer life, but slightly less wettability. Boat manufacturers tune this ratio for different applications — film metallising, paper metallising, capacitor film — and this is one of the key things to understand when comparing boats from different suppliers.

💧 Wettability — The Most Important Property Nobody Talks About

Ask ten metalliser operators what makes a good boat and most will say "life" or "no cracking." Rarely will anyone mention wettability — but wettability is the single property that most directly determines coating quality, and indirectly determines boat life.

Wettability describes how well molten aluminium spreads across the boat's cavity surface when it melts. Good wettability = molten aluminium spreads evenly across the full cavity area, forming a thin, uniform liquid film that evaporates consistently. Poor wettability = molten aluminium beads up, forms puddles, does not spread — the evaporation is uneven, the aluminium may spit or splash, and the coating on the film will be patchy or have optical density variations.

The tradeoff that Supervac and other quality boat manufacturers are very clear about: higher wettability comes at the cost of corrosion resistance. A boat with excellent wettability allows the molten aluminium to spread and react more actively with the BN in the boat surface — which gradually corrodes and erodes the cavity. A boat optimised for maximum life (high BN, better corrosion resistance) will have slightly lower wettability. The best boats are engineered to balance both — and maintaining good wettability throughout the boat's service life, even as the cavity surface erodes slightly, is the mark of a quality product.

On the shop floor, poor wettability shows up as: aluminium puddles in the boat cavity instead of spreading, spitting or spattering of aluminium droplets, uneven OD (optical density) across the film width, and streaky coating that looks like some boats are working harder than others.

💀 Why Boats Fail — The Six Real Causes

Six ways a boat can fail — and the root cause behind each one. Most failures are preventable with the right procedures.

Failure 1 — Thermal shock cracking on first use. New boats always contain some residual moisture absorbed during storage. If a new boat is powered up too quickly — full current applied immediately — the moisture inside converts to steam faster than it can escape through the boat's pores. The pressure buildup cracks the boat from the inside, often within the first few minutes. Prevention: always heat new boats slowly on first use — start at 50–75% power and ramp up gradually over 15–20 minutes before first use.

Failure 2 — Cracking from the dry/wet zone temperature difference. When the aluminium wire melts and wets only part of the boat cavity (incomplete wetting), the wetted area is cooled by the evaporating aluminium while the dry area continues to heat unchecked. The temperature difference between the wetted zone and the dry zone creates enormous thermal stress. Cracks initiate at the boundary between wet and dry zones, propagate through the boat body, and the boat splits. Prevention: ensure full wetting of the boat cavity from the start of each run. Place aluminium wire in U-shape in the cavity before closing the chamber.

Failure 3 — Puncture from overheating. If wire feed rate is too low for the power being applied, or if the wire feed stops momentarily while power remains on, the boat temperature spikes dramatically. Molten aluminium becomes superheated and reacts aggressively with the BN in the boat — corroding rapidly through the base of the cavity. This causes a puncture — a hole through the boat floor — which ends the boat's life immediately and, if molten aluminium falls onto the copper clamp, can cause a short circuit and machine damage. Prevention: always maintain wire feed rate matched to power level. Never allow power without wire feed.

Failure 4 — Electrode/clamp contact failure. The boat sits in copper electrode clamps. If the clamp surfaces are rough, misaligned, oxidised, or have aluminium deposits on them, current distribution across the boat ends becomes uneven. The ends of the boat see more current than the centre, creating hot spots at the edges. These hot spots cause premature cracking or localised corrosion at the boat ends — the boat appears to have a much shorter life than it should. Prevention: clean copper clamp contact surfaces before every boat change. Check alignment. Replace clamps when they show significant wear or aluminium build-up.

Failure 5 — Sludge build-up blocking the cavity. Over time, oxidised aluminium compounds, aluminium nitride, and other reaction products accumulate as a grey-black sludge in the boat cavity and around its edges. This sludge reduces the effective wetting area, increases local temperature at unwetted zones, and causes uneven evaporation. Left unmanaged, sludge accelerates all other failure modes. Prevention: clean sludge from boat cavities regularly — at every roll change if running hard, at minimum every few hours of operation.

Failure 6 — Air exposure while hot. If the vacuum chamber is vented to atmosphere while boats are still at operating temperature — due to a power cut, emergency stop, or procedural error — the hot boat surface reacts with oxygen in the air. The TiB₂ surface oxidises, the cavity surface degrades, and wettability drops sharply. The boat may physically look fine but will perform poorly thereafter. Prevention: after stopping wire feed and power, wait at least 2 minutes before venting the chamber — the boats must cool below red heat before air contact.

⏱️ How to Extend Boat Life — Seven Practical Rules

These are not theoretical guidelines — they are the practices that separate a plant getting 80 hours per boat from one getting 200+ hours from the same boat, same machine, same aluminium wire.

Rule 1 — Slow heat on every new boat. Start every new boat at 50–75% power. Ramp up to full power over 15–20 minutes during the first production run. This drives out residual moisture without cracking. Do not skip this even under production pressure.

Rule 2 — Pre-wet the cavity before closing the chamber. Before closing the vacuum chamber at the start of every run, place 1–3 pieces of aluminium wire (longer than two-thirds of the boat cavity length) in the cavity in a U-shape. This ensures the cavity wets uniformly from the very first seconds of production, preventing the dry/wet zone cracking described earlier.

Rule 3 — Keep wire feed rate matched to power. Every boat has an ideal relationship between wire feed rate and electrical power. Running too little wire at too much power means the boat gets superheated in the unwetted zones — accelerating corrosion and cracking. Your machine's settings sheet or the boat supplier will give you the recommended wire feed rates for different power levels. Follow them.

Rule 4 — Clean sludge regularly. Grey-black sludge in the boat cavity is aluminium nitride and oxidised aluminium compounds. It reduces wetting area and creates hot spots. Clean it out at every roll change. A simple wooden or ceramic scraper (never metal — metal scratches the boat surface) is all you need. Clean clamp faces at the same time.

Rule 5 — Never power off abruptly — stop wire first, then power. At the end of every run, stop the aluminium wire feed first. Then reduce power to zero. Then wait at least 2 minutes for the boat to cool below visible red heat before venting the chamber. Abrupt power-off while aluminium is still molten can leave a cold aluminium plug in the cavity that stresses the boat on the next heat-up.

Rule 6 — Check clamp contact quality at every boat change. Worn, pitted, or aluminium-contaminated clamp surfaces cause uneven current distribution — which means uneven heating — which means premature cracking. Cleaning clamp faces takes two minutes. Replacing a boat takes much longer. Do the maths.

Rule 7 — Log every boat's life hours. The single most useful data point for optimising boat purchasing and scheduling is the actual hours-per-boat you are achieving, broken down by boat supplier, boat grade, machine position, and operator. Without this log, you are guessing. With it, you can identify which positions on the machine kill boats fastest, which supplier is delivering the best real-world life, and when boat performance is degrading before it causes a quality problem.

🔌 Boat कैसे काम करता है — Electricity, Heat, और Evaporation

Boats क्यों जैसे behave करते हैं वैसे — ये समझने के लिए basic physics समझनी होगी। Evaporation boat essentially एक high-temperature resistor है। Copper electrode clamps boat के दोनों ends को grip करते हैं और high current pass करते हैं — typically 400 से 900 amperes, 5 से 15 volts पर। Boat की ceramic material की carefully controlled electrical resistance है। ये resistance electrical energy को heat में convert करती है — electric heater के wire की तरह, लेकिन far more extreme scale पर। Full power apply होने के seconds में पूरा boat 1400–1500°C पहुँच जाता है।

Current (400–900A) ceramic boat से flow करती है → 1400–1500°C तक heat होती है → aluminium wire touch होते ही melt होती है → evaporate होती है → film पर mirror coating deposit होती है — सिर्फ़ कुछ सौ angstroms thick।

Aluminium wire — typically 1.5 से 2mm diameter — एक spool से controlled rate पर hot boat के centre cavity पर feed होती है। Boat surface touch करते ही melt होती है। Molten aluminium boat की cavity surface पर spread होता है — इस spreading को wetting कहते हैं — और heat इसे evaporate कर देती है aluminium vapour के cloud में। वो vapour ऊपर vacuum में travel करता है और boat zone के ऊपर से pass हो रही BOPET या BOPP film पर land करता है — वो thin metallic coating बनाता है जो हर shiny food pouch पर दिखती है।

Typical metalliser में machine की full width across एक row में कई boats होते हैं — commonly 10 से 20 boats side by side — ताकि पूरी film width uniformly coat हो। हर boat independently अपने clamp pair से powered होता है — operators individual boats की power adjust कर सकते हैं।

🧱 Material — TiB₂ + BN ही क्यों, और कुछ नहीं

Evaporation boats के लिए TiB₂ और BN का choice arbitrary नहीं है। ये decades के development का result है जिसने हर दूसरे candidate material को eliminate किया।

TiB₂ electrical conductivity और high-temperature strength handle करता है। BN thermal shock resistance और chemical stability handle करता है। साथ मिलकर ऐसा material बनाते हैं जिसे industry में कोई replicate नहीं कर सकता।

TiB₂ — Titanium Diboride (conductor)। TiB₂ एक hard, dense ceramic है जिसका melting point 2980°C है — boat के operating temperature का लगभग दोगुना। ये electrically conductive है — इसीलिए boat में current pass करके self-heat हो सकता है। TiB₂ के बिना ceramic insulator होती और resistively heat नहीं हो सकती।

BN — Boron Nitride (shock absorber)। BN एक layered ceramic है — hexagonal structure, graphite जैसा — electrically non-conductive लेकिन exceptional thermal shock resistance वाला। हर metallising run में boat 1400°C तक heat और room temperature तक cool होता है। BN के बिना boat कुछ cycles में ही crack हो जाता। BN microscopic level पर lubricant की तरह भी काम करता है — thermal expansion और contraction को stress fractures के बिना allow करता है।

Ratio matter करता है। TiB₂ और BN का ratio boat का character decide करता है। ज़्यादा TiB₂ = better conductivity, better wettability, लेकिन molten aluminium से ज़्यादा corrosion। ज़्यादा BN = better thermal shock resistance, longer life, लेकिन slightly कम wettability। Boat manufacturers अलग-अलग applications के लिए ये ratio tune करते हैं — film metallising, paper metallising, capacitor film — और यही एक key thing है जो different suppliers के boats compare करते वक्त समझनी होती है।

💧 Wettability — सबसे Important Property जिसकी कोई बात नहीं करता

दस metalliser operators से पूछें एक अच्छा boat क्या बनाता है — ज़्यादातर "life" या "no cracking" बोलेंगे। Wettability की बात शायद ही कोई करे — लेकिन wettability वो single property है जो coating quality को सबसे directly determine करती है, और indirectly boat life को भी।

Wettability describe करती है कि molten aluminium boat की cavity surface पर melt होने पर कितनी अच्छी तरह spread होती है। अच्छी wettability = molten aluminium पूरी cavity area में evenly spread होता है, thin uniform liquid film बनाता है जो consistently evaporate होती है। खराब wettability = molten aluminium bead up होता है, puddles बनाता है, spread नहीं होता — evaporation uneven होती है, aluminium spit या splash हो सकता है, film पर coating patchy होगी।

Supervac और quality boat manufacturers clearly बताते हैं: higher wettability corrosion resistance की कीमत पर आती है। Excellent wettability वाला boat molten aluminium को ज़्यादा actively spread और boat surface के BN से react करने देता है — जो gradually cavity को corrode करता है। Maximum life के लिए optimised boat (high BN, better corrosion resistance) slightly कम wettability होगी। Best boats दोनों को balance करने के लिए engineered होते हैं।

Shop floor पर poor wettability इस रूप में दिखती है: boat cavity में aluminium puddles, spitting या spattering, film width पर uneven OD, streaky coating।

💀 Boats Fail क्यों होते हैं — छह Real Causes

छह तरीके जिनसे boat fail हो सकता है — और हर एक के पीछे root cause। ज़्यादातर failures सही procedures से preventable हैं।

Failure 1 — पहले use पर thermal shock cracking। New boats में हमेशा storage के दौरान absorbed residual moisture होती है। अगर new boat को too quickly power up किया — full current immediately — अंदर की moisture steam में convert होती है जो pores से escape करने से पहले pressure buildup करती है। Boat अंदर से crack हो जाता है, अक्सर पहले कुछ minutes में। Prevention: हर new boat को slowly heat करें — 50–75% power से शुरू करें, 15–20 minutes में gradually ramp up करें।

Failure 2 — Dry/wet zone temperature difference से cracking। जब aluminium wire boat cavity के सिर्फ़ part में wets (incomplete wetting), wetted area evaporating aluminium से cool होता है जबकि dry area heat होता रहता है। Temperature difference से enormous thermal stress बनता है। Cracks wet और dry zones की boundary पर शुरू होकर boat body में propagate होते हैं। Prevention: हर run की शुरुआत से full cavity wetting ensure करें। Chamber close करने से पहले U-shape में aluminium wire cavity में रखें।

Failure 3 — Overheating से puncture। अगर applied power के लिए wire feed rate बहुत कम है, या wire feed momentarily रुक जाती है जबकि power on रहती है, boat temperature dramatically spike करता है। Molten aluminium superheated हो जाता है और BN के साथ aggressively react करता है — cavity base को rapidly corrode करता है। Puncture होता है — boat floor में एक hole — जो immediately boat की life end करता है। Prevention: हमेशा wire feed rate को power level से match रखें। Power on रहते wire feed कभी बंद न हो।

Failure 4 — Electrode/clamp contact failure। Boat copper electrode clamps में बैठता है। Clamp surfaces rough, misaligned, oxidised, या aluminium deposits वाले हों तो boat ends में current distribution uneven होती है। Boat ends ज़्यादा current देखते हैं, edges पर hot spots बनते हैं। ये premature cracking या localised corrosion cause करते हैं। Prevention: हर boat change पर copper clamp contact surfaces clean करें। Alignment check करें।

Failure 5 — Sludge build-up cavity block करना। Time के साथ oxidised aluminium compounds, aluminium nitride, और दूसरे reaction products grey-black sludge के रूप में boat cavity में accumulate होते हैं। ये effective wetting area reduce करता है, unwetted zones पर local temperature increase करता है, और uneven evaporation cause करता है। Prevention: boat cavities से sludge regularly clean करें — hard running में हर roll change पर।

Failure 6 — Hot रहते air exposure। अगर boats operating temperature पर हों और vacuum chamber vent हो जाए — power cut, emergency stop, या procedural error से — hot boat surface oxygen से react करती है। TiB₂ surface oxidise होती है, cavity surface degrade होती है, wettability sharply drop हो जाती है। Prevention: wire feed और power stop करने के बाद कम से कम 2 minutes wait करें — chamber vent करने से पहले boats red heat से नीचे cool हों।

⏱️ Boat Life कैसे Extend करें — सात Practical Rules

सात rules जो 80 hours per boat और 200+ hours per boat के बीच का फ़र्क हैं। सब operational। कोई extra cost नहीं। बस procedure।

Rule 1 — हर new boat पर slow heat। हर new boat 50–75% power से शुरू करें। पहले production run में 15–20 minutes में gradually full power तक ramp up करें। Production pressure में भी skip मत करें।

Rule 2 — Chamber close करने से पहले cavity pre-wet करें। हर run की शुरुआत में chamber close करने से पहले 1–3 pieces aluminium wire (boat cavity length के दो-तिहाई से longer) U-shape में cavity में रखें। ये ensure करता है कि production के पहले seconds से ही cavity uniformly wet हो।

Rule 3 — Wire feed rate को power से match रखें। हर boat का ideal relationship है wire feed rate और electrical power के बीच। बहुत कम wire, बहुत ज़्यादा power = unwetted zones में boat superheat होता है। Machine की settings sheet या boat supplier recommended wire feed rates देगा।

Rule 4 — Sludge regularly clean करें। Boat cavity में grey-black sludge aluminium nitride और oxidised aluminium compounds है। हर roll change पर clean करें। Wooden या ceramic scraper use करें — metal कभी नहीं। साथ ही clamp faces भी clean करें।

Rule 5 — Abruptly power off मत करें — पहले wire, फिर power। हर run के end में पहले aluminium wire feed stop करें। फिर power zero करें। फिर chamber vent करने से पहले कम से कम 2 minutes wait करें — boats visible red heat से नीचे cool हों।

Rule 6 — हर boat change पर clamp contact quality check करें। Worn, pitted, या aluminium-contaminated clamp surfaces uneven current distribution cause करते हैं — uneven heating — premature cracking। Clamp faces clean करने में दो minutes लगते हैं। Boat replace करने में बहुत ज़्यादा।

Rule 7 — हर boat की life hours log करें। Boat purchasing और scheduling optimize करने के लिए सबसे useful data point है actual hours-per-boat — boat supplier, grade, machine position, और operator के हिसाब से broken down। इस log के बिना आप guess कर रहे हैं। इसके साथ आप identify कर सकते हैं कि machine पर कौन सी position boats को fastest kill करती है।

💡 Key Takeaway: Evaporation boat एक छोटा TiB₂+BN composite ceramic है — TiB₂ electrically conductive बनाता है ताकि 1400–1500°C तक self-heat हो, BN thermal shock resistance देता है सैकड़ों heat cycles survive करने के लिए। सबसे important और least discussed property है wettability — molten aluminium cavity में कितनी अच्छी तरह spread होती है। अच्छी wettability = uniform coating; खराब wettability = patchy OD, spitting। छह failure modes हैं: first use पर thermal shock cracking, dry/wet zone cracking, overheating से puncture, clamp contact failure, sludge build-up, hot रहते air exposure — सभी correct procedures से preventable हैं। Buying में specify करें: density (>3.0 g/cm³), composition (TiB₂:BN ratio), hot-pressed manufacturing, application substrate — सिर्फ़ dimensions नहीं। Seven life rules — slow heat ramp, pre-wet cavity, wire feed match करें, sludge clean करें, wire पहले power बाद, clamps check करें, हर boat log करें — same supplier, same machine पर 80 और 200+ hours के बीच का फ़र्क हैं।
