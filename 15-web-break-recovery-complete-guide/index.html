💥 Web Break on Metalliser — The Complete Recovery Guide

Web breaks are the #1 cause of unplanned downtime on any vacuum metalliser. They're also the most misunderstood defect in our industry. Most plants blame "film quality" and call the supplier. But in real plants running thousands of jumbos a year, 8 out of 10 web breaks are caused by something inside your own plant — tension, alignment, cleanliness, or a parameter someone changed and forgot.

This single guide serves everyone who has to deal with web breaks — from the ITI operator threading the film at 3 AM to the plant manager reviewing utilisation numbers on Monday morning. Jump directly to your role, or read the whole thing if you want the full picture:

LAYER 1 · FOR OPERATORS
👷 Web Phati — Ab Kya Karu?
Hinglish · ITI-level · Phone par padho · Machine ke paas khade hoke bhi padh sakte ho

Raat ke 3:47 baje. Aap machine ke paas hain. Tension alarm bajta hai. Viewing window se dekha — web phati hui hai. Abhi ghabrana mat. Pura section 5 minute me padh sakte ho, aur jo bhi zaruri hai wohi hai.

⛔ Sabse Pehle — E-Stop MAT Dabao

E-Stop sirf asli emergency ke liye hai — aag, koi aadmi fas gaya, ya kuch girne wala hai. Web phatna emergency nahi hai. Machine khud controlled stop kar degi — drives dheere-dheere ruk jayenge, boats standby par, vacuum aur cooling chalu rahenge. Aap sirf dekho ki auto-stop complete ho gayi — fir next step pe jao.

🎯 Sabse Pehle 3 Kaam — Kul 2 Minute

Kaam 1 (30 sec): Auto-stop ruk gayi? Confirm karo. Time note karo — kitne baje break hui.

Kaam 2 (1 min): Phati hui film ke paas jao. Haath mat lagao. Pehle phone se photo lo — tear ka close-up. Yeh photo baad me supervisor ko dikhana hai. Agar tumne clean kar diya, evidence chala gaya.

Kaam 3 (30 sec): Tear ka pattern dekho — seedha hai? Tircha (diagonal) hai? Edge se shuru hua? Niche ki tasveer se match karo.

👀 Tear Dekho — Kya Reason Hai

Har web break ka tear alag dikhta hai. Photo le chuke ho? Ab yeh 5 patterns se match karo. Jo pattern match ho, wohi aapka reason hai.

✂️ Pattern 1 — Seedhi Saaf Tear (Horizontal)

Film ekdum saaf, seedhi lakir me phati. Jaise kisi ne kaenchi se kaata ho.

➤ Reason: Film zyada tight thi (tension zyada thi).
➤ Aap kya karo: Tension display dekho — number stable hai ya upar-neeche jump kar raha hai? Jump kar raha hai toh dancer roller (woh jhoolne wala upper-niche) haath se push karo. Agar chipka hua lagta hai ya nahi ghumta easily, supervisor ko batao.

╱ Pattern 2 — Tircha (Diagonal) + Jhurriya

Tear tircha — kone se kone tak. Phatne se pehle film me jhurriya (wrinkles) aa rahi thi.

➤ Reason: Web side me slide ho raha tha (alignment bigad gayi thi).
➤ Aap kya karo: Web guide ka sensor (jo film ke kinare pe lagi lal LED wala hai) dekho — saaf hai ya dhool hai? Dry cloth se halka saaf karo. Fir threading karte waqt slow speed pe 50 meter chalao aur dekho film kinare se 5mm se zyada hilti toh nahi. Hilti hai to Mechanical ko call karo.

┣ Pattern 3 — Ek Kinare Se Tear Shuru

Tear ek side (left ya right) se shuru hui, bich tak phatti gayi.

➤ Reason: Unwind roll ke kinare pe damage tha. Shayad forklift se thokar lagi, ya supplier se hi damaged aaya.
➤ Aap kya karo: Us roll ko waapas stores bhejo — aage mat chalao. Damaged roll continue karoge toh phir phategi. Agli roll mount karne se pehle 30 second lena — flashlight lekar pura circle ghumake dekho, edge pe koi cut ya dent toh nahi. Yeh 30 second har break se bachayega.

🔥 Pattern 4 — Jali Hui (Burnt) + Kala Dhabba

Tear ke paas chota jala hua nishan, ya kale aluminium ke chhote bindi jaise daag.

➤ Reason: Boat splash — boat se molten aluminium uchchal ke film pe gira aur jala diya.
➤ Aap kya karo: Wahi boat turant retire karo — yani next cycle me nikal do. Boat usage log dekho — agar 80% life cross kar chuki hai, woh boat splash karti hi rahegi. Supervisor ko batao, naya boat lagwao.

∿ Pattern 5 — Uljhi Huii (Random Jagged) Tear

Tear na seedhi, na tirchi — tooti-phooti, uneven. Film pe scratch marks ya dust dikh raha.

➤ Reason: Gande roller ya film me hi defect.
➤ Aap kya karo: Saare idler rollers ko IPA aur lint-free cloth se wipe karo. Agar yeh hi roll pe 2nd time tear hua, toh us batch ke baki rolls hold karo aur supervisor ko dikhao — ho sakta hai film hi kharab hai.

🎯 Agar Pattern Match Nahi Ho Rahi?

Koi baat nahi. 3 cheez check karo ek-ek karke. 30 second lagega bas.

1. Speed change: Abhi-abhi speed badli thi? Bada jump tha? Answer yes toh reason yehi hai — aage se 50 m/min ke steps me badhana, ek dum se 200 m/min jump nahi karna.

2. Static: Sardi hai? Dry weather? Film fluttering kar rahi thi break se pehle? Anti-static bar ka light jal raha hai check karo. Nahi jal raha? E&I ko call.

3. Joint: Jahan tear hui hai wahan koi joint tha? Double-sided tape wala? Joint weak tha shayad. Next joint banate waqt tape achhi tarah dabao, beech me air bubble nahi rehna chahiye.

📝 Re-Threading ke Baad — F/16 Me Likhna Mat Bhulo

Threading ho gayi, machine chalu, shift chal rahi hai. Ab 30 second ka kaam reh gaya hai — F/16 format me entry.

Yeh kyun zaruri hai: 30 din tak har break log hoga. Mahine ke end me supervisor aur engineer saath dekhenge — pattern milega. "Oh, saare breaks Met-2 pe raat ki shift me hote hain" ya "har baar batch 2403-P se". Log nahi karoge = pattern kabhi nahi milega = break hote rahenge.

F/16 me likhne ke liye cause code yaad rakho:

T = Tension  •  E = Edge damage  •  A = Alignment  •  D = Dirty roller  •  S = Speed change  •  St = Static  •  F = Film defect  •  B = Boat splash  •  O = Other

Bas itna likho: "03:47, Met-2, Jumbo GMB26D0047, 480m pe break, Pattern 2 (Diagonal), Cause: A (Alignment), Downtime 52 min, Waste 65m, EPC sensor clean kiya." Bas itna kaafi hai.

🛡️ Agle Break Se Bachne ke liye 5 Chote Kaam

Yeh 5 kaam routine me shamil kar lo — hafte me break 3-4 se 1 pe aa jayega.

  • Roll mount karne se pehle 30 second: Flashlight lekar roll ko ghumao, edge pe cuts ya damage dekho. 30 second = ghanto ki bachat.
  • Shift shuru me tension display check: 30 second stare karo. Stable hai na? Fluctuation dikh raha hai to shift shuru me hi issue raise karo.
  • Har 48 ghante me roller wipe: Coating zone se pehle wale idler rollers ko IPA se wipe karo. Har roller pe last-cleaned date ka sticker.
  • Speed change hamesha 50 m/min step me: 600 se 800 m/min ek click me mat badhao. 50-50 ke steps me, 30-30 second ke hold ke saath.
  • F/16 har break pe bharo: Bhale hi jaldi me ho. 30 second ka kaam hai. Nahi bharoge toh pattern kabhi nahi milega.

⚠️ Why Web Breaks Cost More Than You Think

  • Direct production loss: 45-60 minutes average per break × 2-3 breaks per shift = 2+ hours lost daily
  • Wasted bare film: 40-80 metres scrapped per break (threading loss + damaged section)
  • Chamber vent cycle: Re-pumping vacuum consumes 25-40 min + diffusion pump stress
  • Missed customer deliveries: 3 breaks in one shift = 1 jumbo short = delayed dispatch
  • Operator fatigue: Night-shift threading under pressure = more errors on the next roll
  • Hidden quality cost: Post-break rolls often have OD variation in first 100m — future rejection risk

Add it up. A plant running 2 breaks per shift × 3 shifts × 30 days = 180 breaks per month. At an average of ₹4,000 lost per break (downtime + waste + rework), that's ₹7.2 lakh per month silently leaking from your P&L. Most plants never calculate this because each individual break feels "small".

⛔ DO NOT press E-Stop for a web break.

E-Stop is for real emergencies only — fire, person in danger, entanglement. Pressing E-Stop cuts power to critical systems (boat heating, cooling circuits, winding drives) and can cause boat thermal shock, create new alarms, and make recovery slower. Let the machine's automatic break-detection sequence do a controlled stop: drives ramp to zero, boats drop to standby, vacuum and drum cooling stay ON.

🏭 Real Shop Floor Story — The ₹21 Lakh Bearing

Met-2, night shift, December. The plant had been averaging 4 web breaks per shift for almost 6 months. Every operator had a different theory. Supervisors blamed "bad winter film quality". Production blamed night-shift operators. Night-shift blamed morning-shift for leaving the machine with wrong tension. Nobody agreed, nobody could prove anything, and the breaks kept coming.

Production loss was running at ~8 hours/day of unplanned downtime. Bare film waste had climbed to 2.3% from a 1.1% baseline. Two customer deliveries had gone short. The plant manager was under pressure to explain why metalliser utilisation had dropped from 82% to 68%.

The Quality Head proposed one change: "Har break F/16 mein log hoga. No exceptions. Agar tension fluctuation thi, cause code T, aur tension value bhi likho." Every web break, fully documented. For 30 days.

After 30 days, 97 breaks were logged. The engineer sat down with the data on a Saturday. The pattern was loud:

  • 73% of breaks showed diagonal tears
  • 81% of those had tension fluctuation >±10% in the 30 seconds before the break
  • Nearly all of them were on Met-2

They pulled the dancer roller bearings on Met-2 that weekend. One bearing — the one feeding tension data to the PLC — had developed a 0.3mm radial play. Not enough to make noise. Not enough to show up on PM visual check. But enough to send wrong tension signals to the control loop, which then over-corrected, which created the fluctuation, which led to the diagonal tear. One ₹4,200 bearing was costing the plant ₹21 lakh per month in downtime + waste.

After the bearing change, the break rate on Met-2 dropped from 4/shift to 1/week within 10 days. Six months later it was still stable. The plant now keeps a running dashboard of F/16 data and reviews it every Monday morning. They don't argue about web break causes anymore — they just look at what the data says.

Lesson: Web breaks aren't random events. They're a signal your machine is telling you something. But the signal only becomes readable when you log every single break honestly in F/16. The plants that fix their break rate are the plants that treat every break as data, not as a 45-minute annoyance to move past.

🛡️ Prevention Checklist for Supervisors

  • Weekly bearing audit: Every dancer and EPC bearing gets a manual play check every 7 days. Any perceivable play = replace
  • Load cell calibration: Every 6 months without exception. Log in F/10 or F/11 PM Quarterly
  • Recipe lock on film-type change: Operators cannot change micron setting without supervisor sign-off on SOP-03
  • IPA roller wipe: All idlers before coating zone wiped every 48 hours. Sticker-per-roller with last-cleaned date
  • Ramp protocol training: Every operator trained on the 50 m/min step rule. Verify during skill assessment
  • Boat retirement at 80%: No more "squeeze-the-last-jumbo" on end-of-life boats
  • Edge inspection before mount: 30-second rotation under light. No exceptions. Track in F/03 Job Card
  • Monday morning F/16 review: Quality + Production sit down, look at last week's data, pick one thing to fix

🗓️ The 30 / 60 / 90-Day Roadmap

If you're serious about cutting your web break rate, here's the rollout that works. This is not theoretical — it's pulled from plants that went from 4/shift to <1/week:

Days 1-30: Build the Data Foundation

  • Day 1: Print & laminate the operator Action Card at every metalliser. Announce the F/16 logging rule in all three shifts
  • Days 1-30: Every single web break gets logged in F/16 — no exceptions, no verbal reports. Supervisor verifies completeness at shift handover
  • Week 2: Supervisor does a sanity audit — spot-check 10 F/16 entries, confirm fields filled properly
  • Day 30: Quality + Production sit together, review all 30 days of F/16 data, identify the top pattern (machine, shift, tear signature, cause code)

Days 31-60: Fix the Top Pattern

  • Days 31-35: Engineer investigates the top pattern with targeted inspection — bearings, sensors, cleanliness records, operator SOP compliance
  • Weekend of Day 40: Planned downtime for the fix — bearing replacement, sensor recalibration, whatever the data pointed to
  • Days 41-60: Continue logging. Watch the break rate. If the fix worked, the line on your Monday chart goes down within 10 days

Days 61-90: Institutionalise the Discipline

  • Every Monday morning: 15-minute "F/16 Review" — Quality + Production + maintenance look at last week's data, pick the next pattern to fix
  • Day 75: Formalise the process — write the Monday Review as an SOP, assign permanent owners, make it part of weekly rhythm
  • Day 90: Board-level report showing: baseline break rate → current break rate → ₹ saved → payback achieved

⚠️ The 3 Failure Modes to Avoid

Plants that try this initiative and fail almost always fall into one of three traps:

  1. Logging theatre: F/16 gets filled with copy-paste entries, fake data, or rushed one-word causes. Result: no pattern emerges, initiative dies at Day 45. Fix: Supervisor personally audits 5 random entries every week. Quality signs off. Logging accuracy becomes a KPI.
  2. Fix-of-the-week: Engineer jumps on Day 5 to "fix" something without waiting for 30 days of data. Usually wrong root cause. Rate doesn't improve. Demotivates team. Fix: No fixes until Day 30 F/16 review. Discipline.
  3. Monday review becomes optional: Once break rate comes down, the Monday F/16 review stops feeling urgent. People skip it. Rate creeps back up. Fix: Weekly review is non-negotiable even when break rate is at target. It's the discipline that maintains the gain.
