🔑 The 3-Second Scratch Classification Test

When you spot a scratch on metallised film, stop immediately and look at the scratch itself — not the roll. Answer these three questions in order: (1) Does the scratch expose the PET base film, or just disturb the aluminium layer? (2) Is it one line or a repeating pattern? (3) Does it run in the machine direction or at an angle? These three answers narrow the cause to one of six specific roller stations.

Surface ScuffLight haze, visible only under torch at angleAluminium disturbed, PET intactSoft contact — dust or tape
Deep ScratchClear line exposing shiny PET underneathAluminium fully removedHard edge — blade, metal burr
Repeating PatternLines at fixed spacing along lengthMatches roller circumferenceDefect stuck on a roller
Random ScatteredShort lines in unpredictable positionsNo repeating patternContamination pickup

⚠️ Why Scratches Cost More Than You Think

A scratch is the single most visible defect on metallised film. Unlike pinholes (hidden until lamination) or low OD (measurable only with a sensor), scratches are obvious under any light source. Every brand owner QC team runs a torch-light inspection before acceptance — and scratches are the #1 rejection reason globally. The real cost breakdown:

  • Customer rejection: Full roll to B-grade = ₹45,000 to ₹80,000 loss per roll
  • Export dispatch reversal: Freight cost both ways + reputation damage
  • Audit findings: Repeating scratches flag systematic process failure
  • Hidden multiplier: One unnoticed scratched roller keeps producing defects until caught

A single hour of undetected scratching at 800 m/min = 48,000 metres of contaminated film. That is 6 to 8 full customer rolls gone, before anyone runs the torch inspection.

🎯 The 6-Station Scratch Detective Walkthrough

Scratches come from physical contact between film and something harder, dirtier, or misaligned. The metalliser-to-slitter chain has exactly 6 scratch-risk stations. Walk them in this order, and you will find the guilty roller in under 10 minutes.

The golden rule of scratch detection: start at the rewinder (where the defect is seen) and walk upstream. Never start at the unwinder. The scratch is produced by the most downstream offender — so walking forward wastes your time.

🔬 Scratch Fingerprint Decoder — Spacing Tells You Which Roller

Here is the detective trick that 90% of operators never learn: measure the distance between repeating scratch marks. That distance equals the circumference of the guilty roller. Every roller in your machine has a unique circumference. Match the spacing to the roller.

Scratch SpacingRoller CircumferenceLikely RollerWhat To Check First
Under 200 mmSmall idler (60-65 mm dia)Guide idler, dancer rollerRubber sleeve damage
300-500 mmMedium roller (100-160 mm dia)DTR roller, draw rollerEmbedded particles in rubber
600-900 mmNip roller (200-285 mm dia)Slitter nip, lay-on nipGlazing, contamination
Over 1500 mmLarge drum (480+ mm dia)Chill drum, process drumAluminium pickup, tape edge
No repeatStatic object in web pathFixed bar, broken sensorVisual inspection at angle

Measurement technique: Mark two clear scratch points with a marker on a sample piece. Use a measuring tape along the machine direction. The distance between them is the roller's circumference. Divide by π (3.14) to get diameter, then match to your roller specs.

🛡️ Prevention — 8 Routines That Stop Scratches Before They Start

  • Pre-shift IPA wipe-down: Every idler, every spreader, every nip — wipe with IPA-soaked lint-free cloth before first roll of the shift
  • Torch inspection every 2 hours: Pull a sample from rewinder, inspect under 300-lumen torch at 45° angle
  • Nip roller log book: Record every clean, replace, and contamination finding — spot patterns early
  • Masking tape check: Chill drum edge tape changed every shift — curled edges are scratch sources
  • Rubber sleeve audit: Monthly hardness check with durometer, replace glazed sleeves
  • No tape on rollers: Never repair rollers with electrical tape or duct tape — residue migrates onto film
  • Controlled cleaning agents: IPA only for metallised surfaces — never mineral spirit, thinner, or petrol
  • Spreader bar cleaning: Bowed spreaders collect dust in their curve — clean carefully every shift

One plant implemented these 8 routines and reduced scratch-related B-grade downgrades by 73% in 90 days. Not one of the routines costs money. Every one of them takes less than 5 minutes.
